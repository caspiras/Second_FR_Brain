# Bullet Writing Guide

All todos and accomplishments in this journal must follow a concise bullet format optimized for impact and clarity.

## The Rules

### 1. Maximum 20 Words
Keep your bullets concise. Every word should add value.

**✗ Bad:** "Successfully led a cross-functional team of 5 talented engineers to complete the critical infrastructure upgrade project ahead of the original schedule and significantly under the allocated budget"

**✓ Good:** "Led team of 5 engineers to complete infrastructure upgrade ahead of schedule and under budget" (16 words)

### 2. No Semicolons (;)
Semicolons are not allowed. Use commas, "and", or split into separate bullets.

**✗ Bad:** "Completed project; delivered on time"

**✓ Good:** "Completed project and delivered on time"

### 3. No Colons (:)
Don't use colons. Rephrase your sentence.

**✗ Bad:** "Project outcome: exceeded all expectations"

**✓ Good:** "Project exceeded all expectations"

### 4. No Dashes (-)
Avoid hyphens and dashes. Spell out compound words or rephrase.

**✗ Bad:** "Completed high-priority project successfully"

**✓ Good:** "Completed high priority project successfully"

## Strong Action Verbs

Start your bullets with powerful action verbs:

### Leadership
- Led, Managed, Directed, Supervised, Coordinated, Organized, Guided

### Achievement
- Achieved, Accomplished, Completed, Delivered, Executed, Implemented, Established

### Improvement
- Improved, Enhanced, Optimized, Streamlined, Reduced, Increased, Accelerated

### Creation
- Developed, Created, Designed, Built, Engineered, Authored, Produced

### Problem Solving
- Resolved, Diagnosed, Troubleshot, Fixed, Corrected, Addressed

## Include Metrics

Quantify your accomplishments when possible:
- Numbers (5 team members, 50 servers, 100 users)
- Percentages (40 percent improvement, 25 percent reduction)
- Timeframes (2 weeks early, 3 months ahead of schedule)
- Money (saved $10K, under budget by 15 percent)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Too Wordy
✗ "I successfully worked with my team to complete the project before the deadline"
✓ "Led team to complete project ahead of deadline"

### Weak Verb
✗ "Helped with the deployment process"
✓ "Coordinated deployment process across 3 environments"

### No Impact
✗ "Worked on improving the system"
✓ "Improved system performance by 45 percent through code optimization"

### Task-Focused Instead of Accomplishment-Focused
✗ "Attended meetings and reviewed code"
✓ "Conducted code reviews improving code quality by 30 percent"

## Quick Tips

1. **Past tense** for completed items
2. **Specific numbers** over vague terms
3. **Impact over activity** (what changed, not what you did)
4. **One idea per bullet** (don't cram multiple accomplishments together)
5. **Active voice** (not passive)

## Before You Submit

Ask yourself:
- [ ] Is it 20 words or less?
- [ ] Does it start with a strong action verb?
- [ ] Does it include a metric or specific outcome?
- [ ] Have I removed all semicolons, colons, and dashes?
- [ ] Does it showcase impact, not just activity?
